HISTORY OF THE CLUB

About

Manchester United Football Club is a professional football club based in Old Trafford, Greater Manchester, England, that participates in the Premier League, the highest tier of English football. The club, nicknamed the Red Devils, was founded in 1878 as Newton Heath LYR Football Club, but changed its name to Manchester United in 1902. In 1910, the club relocated from Newton Heath to its current stadium, Old Trafford.

Manchester Unted are regarded as one of the most supported teams in the world. Their main rivals are Liverpool and,  more recently, Manchester City. In the past, they also rivaled Arsenal and Leeds Unite. The Manchester United and Liverpool is considered to be one of the biggest rivalrieof all time, after years of competeting against eachother to win the some of the biggest competitions in the world.

IRISH PLAYERS

Some of Ireland's most successful football players have spent many year representing the red and white of Manchester United. Some of these players include Paul McGrath, Roy Keane, Dennis Irwin and John O'Shea. Keane and John O'Shea have experience in captaining this side in multiple games throughout the Premier League. Dennis Irwin captained United on his last game for the club as Alex Ferguson granted it to him.              

ALEX FERGUSON

Sir Alexander Ferguson CBE (born December 31, 1941) is a Scottish former football manager and player best remembered for his tenure as manager of Manchester United from 1986 to 2013. He is largely recognized as the greatest football manager of all time, with more trophies than any other manager in football history.
